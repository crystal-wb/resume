About Me

Hello, it’s nice to meet you. I’m Crystal Janeen Williams-Brown and I am an artist/programmer/gamer. My life’s journey is a bit of a strange one, so on paper I don’t come across well. But in person you can see how all the seemingly random bits of my experience have coalesced into a capable, funny, friendly and kind human being. There have been many challenges, miserable circumstances, and heart breaks. However, I don’t regret any of it. I am grateful for both the pain and joy that has made me who I am. I have a saying, “Like flowers, people need both sunshine and rain to grow.”

Okay, enough of that dreary stuff. I am obsessed with video games. Oh, that segway doesn’t make sense? Well that’s how I talk. My friends used to make a game out of mapping my train of thought. Even I find it difficult to follow at times, still it’s never boring. Besides my entire life is made of weird segwways. Anyway, video games have been a part of my life for a very long time. My mom would get me to behave in school by granting or taking away my privilege to play games. At the time I didn’t even have my own game platform to play on, I would play at her best friend’s house (which was also the home of my best friend-talk about kismet) and during the summer I could play my cousin’s consoles. I didn’t get my own until I was heading towards middle school. That is when I got my first Gameboy. I seriously slept next to it every night. Later I saved up any money I got until I could finally buy an Xbox in high school along with a Nintendo 64. The most money and time I have ever spent has been on video games and computers (which I upgraded so I could play better video games).

Despite this love, I thought I was too dumb to learn programing. Even after doing really well in a computer repair class, I thought creating the content I loved so much was out of my reach. This is because I was told I was dumb from elementary school through high school. I was placed in special classes and made fun of regularly. At the time I had no idea that my learning style was just different. It wasn’t until college (which I only attended for my mom’s sake) that I began to see how smart I could be. I went from a C being a high grade for me to getting A’s and A+’s constantly. I graduated from Wake Tech with a 3.9, which shocked me. Still I would only look at the students taking programing courses and talk to them about the courses with envy, while avoiding taking a single programming class myself. My classes where all humanities focused, so I thought I was only smart when it came to studying people or sciences such a biology and geology (I’ve been in love with rocks since I was little, mostly because they allow me to see the ‘code’ that things are made of).

Though I had at last discovered I could be smart, I hadn’t figured out what to do with my intelligence. After some stressful, shady and miserable jobs I ended up applying to an art school (University of North Carolina School of the Arts, specifically)-since I knew I loved art. There my friends dragged me to an animation club where I instantly fell in love with the concept of making something from nothing. It was tedious, difficult and exhausting. Yet, I didn’t want to stop doing it. All my friends quit the club but I remained and joined the animation program. However, as I went deeper into animation I realized something was still missing. I couldn’t put my finger on it. After graduating I struggled to find work, all the while feeling like working for an animation studio wasn’t quite right for me. Then I hit the hardest time of my life: I had no money, I went through hurricane Sandy (I’m from the Jersey Shore), my mom lost her home, and I got extremely ill but couldnâ€™t afford a doctor. During all that games gave me hope/strength.

I began to think of how I used to make up video game stories and decided to see if I could get hired by one of my favorite game companies, Mad Head Games, to write stories. They didn’t have an opening but they did have an extremely kind HR person by the name of Tamara Milenkovic who helped guide me into studying game design. I decided to give it a shot since I had nothing to lose. I took story classes on Coursera (with a scholarship because that was the only way I could afford to do so). Then I started playing around with Unity 3D after watching some Markiplier let’s plays of games created on that engine by people who taught themselves to code. Things clicked. I took a few classes in programming with C# and realized I wanted to know more code, but I also wanted a teacher I could actually talk to if I needed to (one of the downsides of Coursera). The Iron Yard was too expensive but one of their employees (Stacey Vernon) told me about Code The Dream. Now that I’m in the Code The Dream Ruby program I feel like I at last have a sense of where I belong. I am a builder. All my varied experiences allow me to create diverse content. And my strange brain allows me to see the end at the beginning. That’s why I speak in a odd way and that’s why I’m good at what I do. I wouldn’t have it any other way.

Thank you for taking the time to read all this!

Sincerely, Crystal
